# Step 6 — Submission Explanations

## 1. Difficulty Explanation
The task is highly challenging because multi-body orbital resonance integrations require extremely high floating-point precision over long integration horizons. Any small deviation in leapfrog integrator half-steps or precision compensation accumulation (like parenthesization order in Kahan summation) leads to exponential energy drift. In addition, close encounters can trigger gravitational singularities without softening potential parameters, and resonance locking detection is highly sensitive to noise, requiring standard deviation sliding windows.

## 2. Solution Explanation
The simulation state was stabilized by:
- Ordering the Velocity Verlet integrator steps correctly: half-step velocity update, full-step position update using the accumulated velocities, force computation, and the second half-step velocity update.
- Fixing the Kahan precision accumulator parenthesization to `double y = val - comp; double t = sum + y; comp = (t - sum) - y;` to ensure rounding errors are captured.
- Introducing the soft-core softening potential parameter `epsilon_sq = 0.0001` in the gravitational distance computation to avoid division by zero during encounters.
- Implementing a sliding window standard deviation calculation over the past 50 resonance angles in `simulation_engine.cpp` to correctly flag locking (average standard deviation < 1.0).

## 3. Verification Explanation
The test suite drives the C++ simulation and verifies:
- Energy drift conservation under long-term simulations (drift < 1.0e-6).
- Energy conservation across step size transitions in adaptive simulations.
- Prevention of floating-point rounding loss using the Kahan accumulator over many steps.
- Accurate resonance detection locking filter checks.
